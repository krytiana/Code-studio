Burkina Faso, Niger & Mali Form AES Bloc

Posted on May 25, 2025

BREAKING: A new era of unity is unfolding in West Africa as Burkina Faso, Niger, and Mali announce their bold move to merge key national functions under a single alliance — the AES (Alliance of Sahel States).

The alliance is set to feature:

  • A single currency to improve trade and reduce reliance on foreign exchange systems
  • A unified passport for seamless travel and commerce
  • A collective military to enhance regional security
  • Unrestricted movement of people and goods across borders

While each country will maintain its own president and internal governance, the AES seeks to operate as a coordinated bloc. This historic decision could mark a major shift in regional politics, economic integration, and African self-determination.

"This isn’t just about unity — it’s about harnessing power together." — Regional analyst

Many see the AES as a direct response to growing distrust in traditional Western alliances and institutions like ECOWAS, which has seen its influence wane in recent years.

Could AES Be Africa’s Next Superpower?

With shared goals and increasing coordination, the AES has the potential to grow into a dominant regional force — economically, militarily, and politically. Its success may even inspire similar alliances across the continent.
